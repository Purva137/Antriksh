1963 — 2035+

India's Space
Timeline

From a borrowed sounding rocket launched off a bicycle to a permanent space station — six decades of India reaching further than anyone imagined possible.

THE BEGINNING
1963
MilestoneFirst Sounding Rocket — Thumba, Kerala
India launches its first sounding rocket — a Nike-Apache borrowed from NASA — from Thumba Equatorial Rocket Launching Station. The nose cone arrives by bicycle. Dr Vikram Sarabhai's vision becomes real. India is in space.
1969
MilestoneISRO Founded
Indian Space Research Organisation established on August 15, 1969 — Independence Day. Dr Vikram Sarabhai becomes the first chairman. His vision: space not for prestige but for development.
1975
MilestoneAryabhata — India's First Satellite
April 19, 1975. India's first satellite launches on a Soviet rocket. 26-sided polygon. GN2 attitude control. India enters the space age. The 2-rupee coin will carry this moment forever.
1980
MilestoneRohini / SLV-3 — India's Own Rocket
July 18, 1980. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam leads SLV-3 to orbit. India becomes the 6th nation to launch its own satellite on its own rocket. The foundation of everything that follows.
1981
CommsAPPLE — First Indian Comms Satellite
Transported to its test site on a bullock cart. First Indian geostationary communications satellite. Ancestor of every GSAT and INSAT that followed.
1983
CommsINSAT-1B — India's Telecom & Weather Backbone Begins
First operational multipurpose geostationary satellite. TV relay, telephony, and meteorology in one spacecraft. Doordarshan reaches villages unreachable by terrestrial transmission.
PSLV ERA
1993
MilestonePSLV — First Launch
India's Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle makes its first attempt. Partial failure — but the vehicle that will become India's most reliable rocket, with 55+ consecutive successes, is born.
1999
Earth Obs.Oceansat-1 — India Watches the Indian Ocean
India's first ocean observation satellite. Phytoplankton maps. Sea surface temperature. The beginning of 25 years of continuous Indian Ocean monitoring.
2003
Earth Obs.Resourcesat-1 — India's Agricultural Eye
140 million farm households. National crop monitoring begins. India's food security planning gains a satellite backbone.
2004
CommsEDUSAT — World's First Education Satellite
GSAT-3. The world's first satellite dedicated entirely to education. Live interactive classrooms from a teacher in Bengaluru to students in Leh, Aizawl, and Port Blair simultaneously.
2008
MilestoneChandrayaan-1 — Water on the Moon
India's first lunar mission. M3 instrument discovers water on the Moon — one of the most important lunar findings since Apollo. India plants its flag on the Moon. The water discovery changes lunar science forever.
DEEP SPACE
2013
MilestoneMangalyaan — India Reaches Mars
First Asian country to successfully reach and orbit Mars — and the only country in history to succeed on its first attempt. (ESA also succeeded on first attempt with Mars Express 2003, but as an intergovernmental organisation, not a single country.) Japan's Nozomi (1998) and China's Yinghuo-1 (2011) failed to orbit. India: first attempt, first success. ₹450 crore. 8 years of operation.
2015
ScienceAstroSat — India's Space Telescope
India's first multi-wavelength space observatory. 800+ papers. 20+ countries using data. Oldest UV light ever detected. 10+ years still active. India is doing frontier astrophysics.
2017
World Record104 Satellites — One Rocket
PSLV-C37. February 15, 2017. 104 satellites in 28 minutes from 18 countries — including 88 Dove CubeSats from Planet Labs (USA) alone, the world's largest Earth-imaging constellation. India was the launch provider for one company's entire satellite batch. The world record that made the world pay attention. India is a tier-1 commercial launch nation.
2019
LunarChandrayaan-2 — Orbiter Still Active
Lander crashed. Orbiter survived and thrived. 7+ years active. Most detailed lunar water ice maps ever made. Failure that became a foundation for Chandrayaan-3's success.
GOLDEN DECADE
2023
HistoricChandrayaan-3 — First Nation at Moon's South Pole
August 23, 2023, 6:04 PM IST. Vikram lands. Pragyan rolls out. India is the first nation to land on the Moon's south pole — and the fourth nation ever to achieve a soft lunar landing. National Space Day born.
2023
ActiveAditya-L1 — India at the L1 Lagrange Point
India's first solar observatory. First Indian spacecraft at a Lagrange point. 1.5 million km from Earth, watching the Sun 24/7. VELC at 1.05 solar radii. Space weather guardian.
2024
ActiveXPoSat — 2nd X-ray Polarimetry Mission in History
January 1, 2024. India launches the world's second X-ray polarimetry observatory. POLIX. Thomson scattering. Black hole geometry. Raman Research Institute. India at the frontier of astrophysics.
2025
ActiveNVS-02 — NavIC 2.0 Strengthens India's GPS
January 24, 2025. GSLV-F15 carries NVS-02 to 111.75°E geosynchronous orbit. Second-generation NavIC satellite adds L1 band, indigenous atomic clocks, and replaces aging IRNSS-1E. NavIC now interoperable with every GPS receiver on Earth.
2025
MilestoneSpaDeX — India Becomes 4th Nation to Dock in Space
Early 2025. SDX01 and SDX02 dock in orbit. India joins USSR, USA, and China. NavIC relative navigation. Active-passive docking. The prerequisite for Chandrayaan-4 and BAS is proven.
2025
ActiveNISAR — ISRO × NASA · Most Expensive Earth Obs. Satellite
$1.5 billion. 12m deployable mesh antenna. 80 TB/day. L-band (NASA) + S-band (ISRO). India as launch provider for NASA. Millimetre-precision surface deformation mapping every 12 days.
WHAT'S NEXT
2026
Active · On PadGaganyaan G1 — Uncrewed Test Flight
G1 uncrewed mission on the launch pad for late 2026. Crew module, abort system, and re-entry sequence all tested without crew. Vyommitra humanoid aboard. The final dress rehearsal before India sends humans to space.
2028
UpcomingGaganyaan H1 — India's First Crewed Mission
First crewed Gaganyaan flight targeted for 2028. Four astronauts in training. Second batch (military + civilian STEM) selection begun early 2026. India joins the human spaceflight club — the beginning of everything that follows.
2027
UpcomingChandrayaan-4 — Lunar Sample Return
India's most complex mission. 5 modules. 2 LVM3 launches. Earth orbit docking + Lunar Orbit Rendezvous. ~3 kg samples from the south pole — first south pole samples in history. India becomes the 4th nation to return lunar samples.
2028
UpcomingShukrayaan — India Goes to Venus
India's first Venus mission. Aerobraking. VIRAL instrument (ISRO × Roscosmos × France). SAR radar through clouds. Phosphine investigation. India adds Venus to its interplanetary portfolio.
2028
UpcomingBAS — India's Space Station First Module
Avas core module launches. India begins assembling its own space station. 20 tonnes. 400 km orbit. Indigenous ECLSS. The permanent Indian home in space that Vikram Sarabhai dreamed of.
2035
FutureBAS Fully Operational — India's Permanent Home in Space
Bharatiya Antariksh Station fully assembled and crewed. India has a permanent presence in space — not a visitor to other nations' stations, but the host of its own. From a borrowed sounding rocket in 1963 to a space station in 2035. Seventy-two years. One unbroken will to reach.
